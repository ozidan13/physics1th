Quick Review from Lesson 1

We have learnt that when no resultant force affects the object, it does not move at acceleration. Consequently, when a resultant force acts on the object (ΣF ≠ 0), it moves at acceleration. Accordingly, its velocity changes and it acquires acceleration (a ≠ 0).

Newton defined the factors affecting such acceleration through his second law.

في الدرس اللي فات عرفنا إن لو مفيش قوة محصلة على الجسم (المحصلة = صفر)، فالجسم مش هيتسارع — هيفضل واقف أو يمشي بسرعة ثابتة.

CORE CONCEPT

Newton's Second Law

"The resultant force acting on an object is equal to the time rate of change in the momentum of that body."

OR

"The resultant force acting on an object is equal to the product of the object's mass and its acceleration."
📝 Note

"When a resultant force affects an object, the object acquires an acceleration which is directly proportional to the resultant force and inversely proportional to the object mass."

1- The acceleration is inversely proportional to the mass of the object at constant the resultant force.

2- The acceleration is directly proportional to the resultant force acting on the object at constant the mass of the object.

$$ F = \frac{\Delta P}{\Delta t} = m \cdot a $$
القانون الثاني بيقول: القوة المحصلة = الكتلة × العجلة
يعني لو عاوز تعرف الجسم هيتسارع بقد إيه، اقسم القوة على الكتلة!
ولو عاوز تعرف القوة المطلوبة، اضرب الكتلة في العجلة!
Symbol Name Unit Meaning
F Resultant Force Newton (N) Total net force on the object
m Mass Kilogram (kg) Amount of matter
a Acceleration m/s² Rate of velocity change
ΔP Change in Momentum kg·m/s Change in (mass × velocity)
Δt Time interval s (seconds) Duration of force application
RELATIONSHIPS

How F, m, and a Are Related

Force ↑ → Acceleration ↑

At constant mass, acceleration is directly proportional to the resultant force.

Push harder → Accelerate faster!

Push a shopping cart gently → slow acceleration. Push hard → fast acceleration.

Mass ↑ → Acceleration ↓

At constant force, acceleration is inversely proportional to the mass.

Heavier → Harder to accelerate!

Empty cart → easy to push. Loaded cart → much harder with same push.

Three Ways to Use F = ma

Find Force

$F = m \times a$

Find Acceleration

$a = \dfrac{F}{m}$

Find Mass

$m = \dfrac{F}{a}$

• عاوز القوة؟ اضرب الكتلة في العجلة.
• عاوز العجلة؟ اقسم القوة على الكتلة.
• عاوز الكتلة؟ اقسم القوة على العجلة.

What Newton's 2nd Law Tells Us

  • Moving or stopping a heavy train is much more difficult than a light bicycle (more mass → more force needed for same acceleration).
  • Any object that accelerates must have a net force acting on it.
  • When an object falls, it accelerates at $g ≈ 9.8\;\text{m/s}^2$ — this means gravity is a force ($F_g = mg$).
• القطر الكبير صعب تحركه أو توقفه مقارنة بالعجلة — لأن كتلته أكبر بكتير.
• أي جسم بيتسارع لازم يكون فيه قوة محصلة بتأثر عليه.
• الجسم اللي بيسقط بيتسارع بعجلة الجاذبية g — وده معناه إن الجاذبية دي قوة (الوزن = mg).
SOLVED EXAMPLES

Step-by-Step Solutions

Example 1 — Comparing Two Objects

Two equal forces act on two objects: the first has mass 10 kg and moves with acceleration 5 m/s². The second has mass 25 kg. What is the acceleration of the second object?

Given: $m_1 = 10\;\text{kg}$, $a_1 = 5\;\text{m/s}^2$, $m_2 = 25\;\text{kg}$, Forces are equal ($F_1 = F_2$)

Find: $a_2 = ?$

Solution:

Since $F_1 = F_2$ → $m_1 a_1 = m_2 a_2$

$$\frac{m_1}{m_2} = \frac{a_2}{a_1} \quad\Rightarrow\quad a_2 = a_1 \times \frac{m_1}{m_2} = 5 \times \frac{10}{25} = \boxed{2\;\text{m/s}^2}$$

لما القوة واحدة على جسمين مختلفين في الكتلة، الجسم الأثقل هيتسارع أقل.
الجسم اللي كتلته 25 kg (أكبر 2.5 مرة) هيتسارع 2 m/s² بدل 5 — يعني أقل 2.5 مرة!

Example 2 — Finding Force

What force is required for a car of mass $1.6 \times 10^3$ kg to move with a positive acceleration of 2 m/s²?

Given: $m = 1.6 \times 10^3\;\text{kg} = 1600\;\text{kg}$, $a = 2\;\text{m/s}^2$

Find: $F = ?$

Solution:

$$F = m \times a = 1600 \times 2 = \boxed{3200\;\text{N} = 3.2 \times 10^3\;\text{N}}$$

Example 3 — Finding Acceleration

A car of mass 5000 kg starts from rest under a force of 4000 N. Calculate the acceleration.

Given: $m = 5000\;\text{kg}$, $F = 4000\;\text{N}$

Find: $a = ?$

Solution:

$$a = \frac{F}{m} = \frac{4000}{5000} = \boxed{0.8\;\text{m/s}^2}$$

عربية كتلتها 5000 kg بتتأثر بقوة 4000 N.
العجلة = القوة ÷ الكتلة = 4000 ÷ 5000 = 0.8 m/s²
يعني كل ثانية سرعتها بتزيد 0.8 m/s!

Exercise 1 — Graph Interpretation

The graph below shows the relationship between acceleration ($a$) and force ($F$) for two objects A and B. Which object has the greater mass? Why?

💡 Hint

Slope = $a/F = 1/m$. Steeper slope → smaller mass. Object B has the greater mass because its line is less steep (smaller slope → larger mass).

Exercise 2 — Force Comparison

A force $F$ acts on an object and accelerates it at acceleration $a$. When another force acts on the same object and accelerates it at $5a$. What do you conclude about the two forces?

💡 Hint

Since $F = ma$ and mass is constant: if $a_2 = 5a_1$, then $F_2 = 5F_1$. The second force is 5 times the first force.
